# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

ScientificState is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, diverse, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy toward other community members

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or pull requests
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scientific Integrity

ScientificState is a tool for rigorous, reproducible science. The following behaviors are
**explicitly prohibited** and will result in a permanent contribution ban:

- **Data manipulation:** Altering, fabricating, or selectively omitting data to produce
  a desired result.
- **Result fabrication:** Reporting results that were not actually obtained.
- **Reproducibility sabotage:** Intentionally withholding methods, parameters, or data
  in a way that prevents independent reproduction of reported results.

Violations of scientific integrity are treated as the most serious category of misconduct
in this community. Reports will be escalated to maintainers immediately.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits,
code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct,
and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests,
discussions, and any other official communication channel — as well as public spaces
when an individual is representing the project or its community.

---

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to
the project maintainers at **conduct@scientificstate.org**.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with respect to the reporter.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these steps when determining consequences:

1. **Correction** — Private written warning with an explanation of the violation.
2. **Warning** — Public or private warning with specific behavioral expectations.
3. **Temporary ban** — Temporary removal from community interaction.
4. **Permanent ban** — Permanent removal for severe, repeated, or scientific integrity violations.
